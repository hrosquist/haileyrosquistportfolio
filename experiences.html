Experiences

Operations Lead — University of Oregon Student Recreation Center

As an Operations Lead, I supervised student staff, supported daily facility operations, and helped create a safe and welcoming environment for patrons. I handled policy enforcement, emergency response, facility checks, and shift coordination while communicating clearly with staff and professional supervisors. I also contributed to hiring, training, and staff support by helping organize interviews, review applications, and update resources. This role strengthened my leadership, problem-solving, accountability, and communication skills.

GIS Mapping Project — Seattle Census Analysis

In my geography coursework, I created census-based maps of King County and the Seattle metropolitan area using R, tidycensus, sf, ggplot2, and tmap. I gathered demographic data, cleaned and joined spatial datasets, created maps, and wrote explanations of the geographic patterns shown in the results. This project helped me practice data visualization, spatial analysis, and communicating technical findings to a general audience. It also strengthened my ability to troubleshoot code and organize project files for GitHub Pages.

Data Ethics Project — Bias in Medical Algorithms

For my data ethics course, I researched how bias in medical algorithms can contribute to unequal healthcare outcomes. I focused on a real-world case where an algorithm underestimated the needs of Black patients because it used healthcare cost as a proxy for illness. I created a newspaper-style visual project and presentation explaining how biased training data can reflect historical inequality. This experience strengthened my ability to connect technical systems with ethics, fairness, and social impact.
